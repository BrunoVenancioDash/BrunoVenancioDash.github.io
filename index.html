I have been a professor at the Department of Informatics and Statistics (INE) at the Federal University of Santa Catarina (UFSC) since 2018, affiliated with UFSC's Graduate Program in Computer Science (PPGCC). I am currently an Associate Professor at INE/UFSC, where I served as Head of the Department from 2021 to 2025. I also lead the Connectionism and Cognitive Science Laboratory team. I have experience in research and teaching in Computer Science, with an emphasis on Algorithms, Optimization, and Theory of Computation.

Research interests: Algorithms, Discrete Optimization, and Theoretical Computer Science.

Research

I am interested in Algorithms, Discrete Optimization, and Theoretical Computer Science. My work spans combinatorial optimization, graph and network clustering (modularity and modularity density), neural-symbolic and agent-based systems, and, more recently, quantum computing and machine-learning applications in geophysics.

I lead the team of the Connectionism and Cognitive Science Laboratory.

Prospective students: those interested in undergraduate research, an undergraduate thesis, or a Master's or Ph.D. degree are welcome to contact me by email at r.santiago at ufsc.br.
